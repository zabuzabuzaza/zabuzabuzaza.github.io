Professional Experience?

I was a structural steel detailer at ELLUC Projects . Here's what I did there ->

Every project, a model of all structural steel elements would be constructed from the received civil and architectural plans from the engineers in Tekla Structures. This includes every column, beam, rafter, purlin, and every connection in between.

Here are some examples of projects I've been heavily involved in:

Exhibition Station, CrossRiver Rail, Brisbane
As part of the Crossriver Rail Project, construction of all steel structures on the platform, underground, and auxiliary strucutres around the station.
Dickson Village, Canberra
Construction of all lower-level steelwork such as awnings, facades, canopies, carpark screens, and upper-level plantroom structures
Barwon State School, Geelong
New administration buildings and upgrades to multiple learning centres
East Gungahlin High School, Canberra
Constructed a new General Learning Building, Gymnasium, and several auxiliary structures

Marking plans for the project were made, which need to convey the specifications of the entire project in a readable format.

Hundreds of assembly drawings, and more than a thousand part drawings per project, were annotated correctly with the specifications required for the manufacturers to get it made. This includes keeping track of the complete set of parts and elements of the project, down to the last M20 bolt.

Don't forget a continuous relay of communication between the engineers for any possible clarification / modifications to the design plans, and between the manufacturers for additional project deliverables, or missing / incorrect information we need to amend.

This was over a 15 month period, where I joined as a part of 3 new trainee drafters (they were on the precast team, I was on steel), learning a completely new software without the main detailing supervisor and trainer for our first few months on the job. We quickly adapted, due to our ability to communicate effectively with our seniors, learning from them without being too annoying, and also due to a fight-or-flight response to most of the senior detailers in the Aus team leaving the company, leaving us as the most experienced detailers left.

As the seniors left, more juniors joined to replace them, and it was up to me (now a senior with a whopping 3 months of experience) to train them, and I did.

By the end, I was part of a variety of projects ranging from warehouses, school buildings, to massive government projects, working with around 20 other people in the Philippines, who we had to communicate and coordinate workloads effectively, as well as handling projects entirely by myself (which was much easier to organise honestly). Outside of technical work inside Tekla Structures, we had to keep project files and deliverables organised and clean, work allocation clear and distinct (to avoid sync issues), and communication between stakeholders presentable and professional.

In the end, the Aus team was ultimately let go as the company was downsizing and refocusing its efforts towards engineering consultation over detailing services. It was a tumultuous time, as it usually is with a small business trying to find its footing in a competitive industry, but the people I worked with all made the 2 hour commute I took each way to work a worthwhile decision.
